What is the problem?

Addiction

Addiction is a disorder of the brain's reward system characterized by compulsive engagement in rewarding stimuli despite harmful consequences. It develops over time from chronically high levels of exposure to an addictive stimulus.

Hidden risks

In social life addicts are associated with something filthy, therefore many diseases stay hidden for a long time and that requires more potential time for the recovery process. So instead of being reactive to your health, we promote proactive approach for decreasing self-harm by being aware how different substances affect your body.

Oldest drug

Alcohol is one of the oldest and most common psychoactive drugs with existing evidence on the negative impact on the cognitive performance and nervous system causing heart disease. Its regular use can become a substitute for solving personal and interpersonal problems. Permanent substance abuse causes addiction resulting in mental or physical health problems.

What is AlcoJon?

With our interactive know-how kit, we promote smart and conscious alcohol consumption with contextual indicators for a better understanding of alcohol influence on a body. The idea is that if you start identifying your drinking patterns then you can master, minimize or tolerate triggers of stress and conflict by effective coping strategies.

Publicly accessible real-time intoxication tests help to understand the alcohol influence on a human body in a specific moment based on a breath sample. Once your blood alcohol content (BAC) is estimated, then you can see how alcohol affects your body. Additionally, the test acts as a prevention tool for obtaining basic information about estimations on the body recovery steps.

Through this voluntary check we shift the focus of a human brain’s rewarding system into a positive flow of self-awareness or “treat yourself good” attitude. With a software add-on (eg Native Web App) a person can track total consumption patterns and based on this analysis set up harm reduction goals. Fulfillment of these goals can be exchanged for a discount voucher of an alternative type of entertainment.

How AlcoJon works?

The Alcojon has a kit and a seperate mobile app:

  • Kit
  • Mobile app

Step 1: User input

Person enters his age, weight, height and gender manually.

Step 2: Analyze user's BAC

Person blows into a built-in breathalyzer to check his blood alcohol concentration (BAC). A display shows your estimated BAC on a display

Step 3: Display user's BAC

Based on user's BAC and manual input the kit calculates:

  • amount of of ethanol in your body
  • how much time your liver needs to process the alcohol
  • how much water is needed to balance the body
  • how many hours of sleep/detox is needed for a body recovery

Step 4: Display hologram

You can choose one of the body organs to display as a hologram in the creative part of the kit. So you click on a button choosing the body organ - brain/heart/liver - a hologram appears showing you the organ with highlighted parts affected by alcohol consumption.

Step 1: You download the app to your phone (QR/online link)

Step 2: In the app you can:

  • sync data from a know-how kit breathalyzer
  • manually add other consumed drinks
  • track budget
  • set up goals

Step 3: Set up goals

If you set up goals, then you automatically participate in the reward system giving you discount vouchers to alternative entertainment services

Who is it for?

Everyone is exposed to developing an addiction as none of us is protected from trauma and/or emotional avoidance of specific situations. But the recovery process varies from person to person as we have different levels of positive coping mechanism strength. So clear analysis of personal triggers and use of harm reduction tools can contribute to strengthening the coping mechanism for an early recovery which becomes a long-term success with time and dedication.

As this product relates to alcohol consumption habits, then we need to consider that at different ages alcohol perception varies:

  1. Youngsters:
    • visualization of harm caused to the brain, heart and liver
    • harm reduction estimations for the body recovery
  2. Students
    • tracking budget
    • discount benefits for alternative entertainment
    • identifying consumption triggers
  3. Young adult
    • self-help tool for tracking and improving day-to-day habits
    • discount benefits for alternative entertainment
  4. Drinkers:
    • identifying the levels of consumption and triggers’ patterns
    • alternative entertainment guide
    • engagement with the market society by getting discounts for alternative entertainment
